[Try it and see](https:&#x2F;&#x2F;blog.plover.com&#x2F;misc&#x2F;try-it-and-see.html)   

I thought about this because of[yesterday&#39;s article about the person who needed to count the 3-colorings of an icosahedron](https:&#x2F;&#x2F;blog.plover.com&#x2F;math&#x2F;colored-icosahedron.html), but didn&#39;t try constructing any to see what they were like.

Around 2015 Katara, then age 11, saw me writing up[my long series of articles about the Cosmic Call message](https:&#x2F;&#x2F;blog.plover.com&#x2F;aliens&#x2F;dd&#x2F;intro.html)and asked me to explain what the mysterious symbols meant. (It&#39;s intended to be a message that space aliens can figure out even though they haven&#39;t met us.)

![This is a
127-by-127 pixel image whose purpose is to wordlessly present and
define a series of 5-by-7 pixel glyphs that represent the digits from
0 through 9, and also a glyph representing an equal sign.](https:&#x2F;&#x2F;proxy-prod.omnivore-image-cache.app&#x2F;0x0,so3mn-Fk5VqtlxLkegnxRxWJiXBnWJ-wmnLXxToMj07I&#x2F;https:&#x2F;&#x2F;pic.blog.plover.com&#x2F;misc&#x2F;try-it-and-see&#x2F;dumas-dutil-p1.png)

I said “I bet you could figure it out if you tried.” She didn&#39;t believe me and she didn&#39;t want to try. It seemed insurmountable.

“Okay,” I said, handing her a printed copy of page 1\. “Sit on the chaise there and _just look at it_ for five minutes without talking or asking any questions, while I work on this. Then I promise I&#39;ll explain everything.”

She figured it out in way less than five minutes. She was thrilled to discover that she could do it.

I think she learned something important that day: A person can accomplish a lot with a few minutes of uninterrupted silent thinking, perhaps more than they imagine, and certainly a lot more than if they don&#39;t try.

I think there&#39;s a passage somewhere in _Zen and the Art of Motorcycle Maintenance_ about how, when you don&#39;t know what to do next, you should just sit with your mouth shut for a couple of minutes and see if any ideas come nibbling. Sometimes they don&#39;t. But if there are any swimming around, you won&#39;t catch them unless you&#39;re waiting for them.
